How to Respond to Leads Fast as a Contractor

TradesOnAutopilot.com · 2026 · 5 min read

The single biggest lever in contractor lead conversion isn't your price, your reviews, or your photos. It's how fast you respond. The odds of converting a lead drop sharply after the first five minutes. After an hour, you're a long shot. After a morning — it's over.

Here's how to make sure you're always first — without hiring anyone or being glued to your phone.

The Five-Minute Rule

Set a standard: every lead gets a response within five minutes, 24 hours a day. Not from you — from your system.

Lead at 2pm on a Tuesday — you're on a job. Your agent texts: "Hey [name], got your message — wrapping up a job right now. I'll call you back in 30 minutes. — [Your name]"

Lead at 9pm on a Friday — you're done for the day. Your agent texts: "Hi [name], thanks for reaching out. We'll call you at 7:30am tomorrow. For emergencies call [number]. — [Your name]"

The customer always gets an immediate reply. You always wake up with a clean list of who to call.

When a new message or form submission comes in from a potential customer, respond immediately based on the time: Business hours (7am–6pm weekdays): "Hi [name], got your message! I'm on a job right now — I'll call you back within 30 minutes. — [Your name]" After hours and weekends: "Hi [name], thanks for reaching out to [Business Name]. We'll call you at 7:30am tomorrow. For true emergencies (burst pipe, gas smell, no heat in winter), call [emergency number] now. — [Your name]" In both cases: log the contact, note the time and issue, create a follow-up task for the next business morning.

What to Say When You Call Back

The automation gets you the first response. The callback closes the job. Start with acknowledgment: "Hey [name], this is [Your name] from [Business] — I saw your message about [their issue]. Sounds like a pain. Let me help you get this sorted."

Then offer two specific times: "I've got a slot tomorrow morning or Thursday afternoon — which works better for you?" Two specific options converts better than "when are you free."

The Compounding Effect

Fast response doesn't just win individual jobs — it builds reputation. When you're consistently first to respond, you get more 5-star reviews mentioning your responsiveness. That attracts more leads. Those leads convert at a higher rate. The contractors winning in competitive local markets right now have systematized this. It's not a personality trait — it's a process.
